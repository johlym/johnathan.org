Number 4 - The "Office Space" Edition  May 13th 2017

FROM THE EDITOR

No TPS Reports Here

Welcome to Week 4! One of the keys to a legit awesome space is sometimes paying someone else to design it. Last week we featured entire buildings which were awesome but this time we're focusing in a bit on where people do work. Each of the spaces in this week's issue were professionally designed to be highly functional and productive without clutter and sadness (I assume that was one of the reasons for expressing rage against a printer in 1999's Office Space).

We're also taking a look at a products that'll instantly boost that awesome-clean vibe and make you the envy of your co-workers. There's so sense in having an awesome area to work if you're working on 'meh' tech.

I'd love to hear what you think of this week's round up! Reply or send an email to hello@readtheminimalist.com or take it to social media at The Minimalist on Facebook or Twitter @readminimalist.

Johnathan at The Minimalist

SPACES

FICC Inc.

FICC, Inc. sought the help of Upsetters Architects to craft them an amazing space with loads of clean, straight lights and bright light-filled rooms. It definitely has that post-modern Japanese feel and the frosted windows are a high-tech take on the Shōji.

gomnml.ist

Free at 68 Claremont

At 68 Claremont in Toronto, you'll find an old factory. Long since converted, the third of the building is now home to the Free space where creatives and brands can do some of their best work without un-necessary distraction.

gomnml.ist

Kinfolk magazine

The Danes are great people and Denmark is a beautiful place. Kinfolk magazine in Copenhagen worked with Norm Architects to create a three-zone office. The front is an open gallery that's exposed to the street, the middle zone is centered around the concept of being a meeting and social space, and the offices in the back are isolated away where the staff can do their best work.

gomnml.ist

Chelsea Office

Inside an old Reynolds Wrap factory, the KiDS Creative office at 541 W 25th in New York, NY (the Chelsea neighborhood) was designed with the idea of complimenting the existing archaic structure while still keeping an open and airy feeling about the place.

gomnml.ist

Nakagawa Masashichi Shoten Omotesando Shop ＆ Office

This is one you won't see very often. Inside this office, some of the workspaces have live trees growing out the middle. If there was ever a better blend of form and function, I have yet to see it.

PRODUCTS

Surface Laptop

The new Surface Laptop is, if nothing else, finally some real design competition for the current aesthetic champion Apple. Starting at $999 and making it's way up to $2199, it's not priced to be budget friendly but it makes up for that in build quality, style, and performance. The high end features a Core i7 processor, 16GB of RAM, and 512GB of extremely fast storage. If you ever told yourself "I want a Mac that runs Windows" this is pretty much it.

gomnml.ist

LG 27UD88-W 27" 4K LED Monitor

It's hard to find but for good reason. This display is considered the flagship by many. If you get the chance to see one in person, you'll immediately know why. 27". 4K resolution. USB 3.0. UBS Type C. Super slim bezel. It's a beautiful display and worth the price.

gomnml.ist

Herman Miller Sayl

If you're butt and back aren't happy, you're not happy. At the same time, it's common knowledge that working while laying in bed is hard. The next best thing is a chair that's both comfortable, functional, and perhaps as a bonus, quite good looking. The Sayl checks all three boxes and comes in a wide variety of colors.

gomnml.ist

UPLIFT Standing Desk

Having a great workspace means having a great desk. Standing desks are all the rage but you can't just get any ol' desk. The UPLIFT desks are fantastic for just about any type of work environment and come in a variety of sizes from just a few feet to six feet and beyond. Mix and match your leg colors with any top, add controls, grommet holes, and more, and you've turned your ho-hum office into a slick, clean command center.

gomnml.ist

TwelveSouth BookArc

Clutter is the bane of anyone looking for a clean, fresh place to work. This is where the BookArc comes in. Propping your MacBook up and out of the way, it reclaims space on your desk and provides an added benefit of being able to put that gorgeous aluminum computing device on display.
